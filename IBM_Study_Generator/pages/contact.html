FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions we receive most often about the project and the technology behind it.

IBM Granite is a family of open, enterprise-grade foundation models developed by IBM Research. The Granite 3.3 model used in this platform is specifically designed for instruction-following, text generation, summarisation, and question-answering tasks. It is trained on carefully curated datasets with full transparency in training data sourcing — making it a responsible AI choice for academic applications. IBM Granite models are available via IBM watsonx.ai and as open-weight models on Hugging Face.
The project is developed as part of IBM University Engagement, demonstrating the application of IBM Granite foundation models in academic settings. The front-end codebase is publicly viewable for educational reference. The AI backend components are powered by IBM watsonx.ai's managed APIs. IBM Granite model weights themselves are released under the Apache 2.0 licence and are freely available for academic and commercial use.
The AI chat feature uses Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to ground every response in the student's own uploaded notes. When you ask a question, the system first searches your document knowledge base for the most relevant passages, then passes those passages as context to IBM Granite 3.3, which synthesises a precise, source-grounded answer. This eliminates hallucinations and ensures responses are always relevant to your course material rather than generic web knowledge.
Absolutely. The platform is designed for students across any discipline — upload your lecture notes, textbook excerpts, or revision summaries and let IBM Granite AI generate quizzes, flashcards, and study plans tailored to that exact content. It works with computer science, business, medicine, law, engineering, and humanities material equally well. If your institution is interested in a formal integration or partnership, please reach out via the contact form above.
The platform is built on a modern, standards-based stack: HTML5, CSS3 with custom design tokens, and vanilla JavaScript for the front end. The AI layer is powered by IBM Granite 3.3 8B Instruct served through IBM watsonx.ai APIs, deployed on IBM Cloud. Data visualisation uses Chart.js 4.4. No front-end frameworks or heavy dependencies are used — keeping the platform fast, accessible, and easy to maintain.
